  • 1.The mean of the sampling distribution of the sample mean is:

    2. An approximation of the mean of the population

    3. Not a good estimate of the population mean

    5. None of the above

  • 2.A 99% confidence interval can be interpreted as:

    2. There is a 1% chance that the true parameter value is outside the interval

    4. Both A and B

    5. None of the above

  • 3.Upper and lower interval limits for a 90% confidence interval for m are, respectively, 180 cm and 220 cm. We would infer from these limits a __________ likelihood that __________.

    3. 90%; the interval from 180 cm to 220 cm contains the true population mean m

    4. Only A and B are correct
